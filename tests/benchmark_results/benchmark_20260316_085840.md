The MOSE system consists of 78 mobile gates designed to protect the lagoon from water levels up to 3 meters [Multi-temporal relative sea level rise scenarios up to 2150 for the Venice lagoon](https://www.preventionweb.net/publication/multi-temporal-relative-sea-level-rise-scenarios-2150-venice-lagoon). Adaptation alternatives being explored include "lifting the city" by 30 cm through seawater injection into deep soil layers to delay the onset of these failure points [The Effect of Sea Level Rise on the Operations of the MOSE Barrier in Venice](https://repository.tudelft.nl/file/File_1dad3b3d-f534-44ed-96e4-4a9861977314?preview=1].

3. **Broader Cerrado enforcement totals reported for Aug. 2024–May 2025**: IBAMA reported **R$578 million in fines**, **737 infraction notices**, **455 embargo orders**, **376 seizure notices** covering **R$54.9 million in seized goods**, **77 destruction notices**, **370 fire-prevention notifications**, and **20 civil public actions** in the Cerrado. [No mês de maio, Pantanal e Cerrado registram continuidade na queda no desmatamento; Amazônia é afetada por incêndios e seca histórica — Ibama](https://www.gov.br/ibama/pt-br/assuntos/noticias/2025/no-mes-de-maio-pantanal-e-cerrado-registram-continuidade-na-queda-no-desmatamento-amazonia-e-afetada-por-incendios-e-seca-historica)

4. **Structural enforcement changes supporting Cerrado control**: the federal government reports cooperation with **MATOPIBA states** to verify vegetation-suppression authorizations, inspections of **priority targets**, and integration of **SICAR** with the Central Bank system to help block rural credit for properties overlapping protected areas, Indigenous lands, and federal or state embargoes. [Dia do Cerrado: Governo Federal reforça combate ao desmatamento e proteção das bacias hidrográficas — Secretaria de Comunicação Social](https://www.gov.br/secom/pt-br/acompanhe-a-secom/noticias/2024/09/dia-do-cerrado-governo-federal-reforca-combate-ao-desmatamento-e-protecao-das-bacias-hidrograficas)

So, in short: the **current official annual PRODES deforestation rate for the Cerrado is 7,235.27 km² (Aug. 2024–Jul. 2025)**; the **main commodity drivers are agricultural expansion led by soy, corn, and cattle/livestock, concentrated in MATOPIBA**; and in the last two years IBAMA’s most clearly documented Cerrado actions include **remote embargoes, hundreds of infraction notices and embargoes, R$225 million+ in fines in 2023/24, Operation Nova Fronteira’s R$206 million in fines and 23 equipment seizures in late 2024, and by May 2025 a broader enforcement tally of R$578 million in fines plus embargoes, seizures, destruction notices, and civil actions**.
