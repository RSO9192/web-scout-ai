## Year-by-year scientific basis and stock-assessment results

### 2022 TAC = 36,000 t
The 2022 TAC was based on **SCRS advice first given in 2020 and reaffirmed in 2021**. Rec. 21-08 explicitly says SCRS recommended in its **2020 advice** that TAC be **maintained at 36,000 t for 2022**, to be reviewed in 2021 using updated abundance indicators, and that SCRS in **2021 confirmed** that examining the updated eastern abundance indicators and projections from the **2017 assessment** provided **no evidence to alter** the 36,000 t advice. [21-08 BFT](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Recs/compendiopdf-e/2021-08-e.pdf)

The underlying 2021 SCRS advice says the Group examined **updated eastern abundance indicators** to evaluate whether the recommended TAC for 2022 should change. It concluded that inspection of the **updated biomass indicators** and the **projections of the 2017 assessment** did **not provide any evidence to alter the current management advice**, so **no change** from **36,000 t** was recommended for 2022. [REPORT OF THE STANDING COMMITTEE ON RESEARCH AND STATISTICS (SCRS)](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Meetings/Docs/2021/REPORTS/2021_SCRS_ENG.pdf)

So the **scientific basis for 2022 was not a new benchmark assessment**, but rather an **indicator-based review** of whether new abundance information was consistent with the 2017 assessment projections. The answer from SCRS was yes: the indicators did not justify changing the TAC. [REPORT OF THE STANDING COMMITTEE ON RESEARCH AND STATISTICS (SCRS)](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Meetings/Docs/2021/REPORTS/2021_SCRS_ENG.pdf) [21-08 BFT](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Recs/compendiopdf-e/2021-08-e.pdf)

### 2023 TAC = 40,570 t
The 2023 TAC marked a major shift: ICCAT moved from ad hoc annual/periodic advice to a **formal Management Procedure (MP)**. Rec. 22-08 states that the TACs for **2023–2025** were set at **40,570 t** “**in accordance with the MP**.” [22-08 BFT](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Recs/compendiopdf-e/2022-08-e.pdf)

The Commission’s MP framework was adopted in 2022 and then amended in 2023 through Rec. 23-07. That recommendation says the MP is to be used to calculate TACs for both western and eastern management areas, and that the Commission adopts TACs based on the MP outcome unless SCRS identifies **exceptional circumstances (ECs)**. It also states the MP’s performance measures were evaluated using **48 simulations/replicates for each of 48 operating models over a 30-year projection**. [23-07 BFT](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Recs/compendiopdf-e/2023-07-e.pdf)

For the eastern stock specifically, ICCAT’s 2022–2023 report says the **three-year TACs from the adopted management procedure are, by design, intended to ensure a high probability of maintaining stock status above BMSY and avoiding overfishing**. The same report gives the stock summary used around that decision: **current reported catch (2022) 35,102 t**, **relative fishing mortality Fcurrent/F0.1 = 0.81 (0.48–1.62)**, and **stock status (2020): overfishing = No**. It also notes that **biomass reference points were not estimated since the 2017 assessment due to uncertainty in recruitment potential**. [ICCAT REPORT 2022-2023 (II)](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/BienRep/REP_EN_22-23-II-2.pdf)

In other words, the **scientific basis for the 2023 TAC** was the newly adopted **MSE-derived management procedure**, informed by the **2022 eastern stock assessment** and associated operating models, rather than a single-point benchmark estimate such as a new MSY catch figure. The management framework’s explicit purpose was to maintain stock status above BMSY with high probability while avoiding overfishing. [23-07 BFT](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Recs/compendiopdf-e/2023-07-e.pdf) [ICCAT REPORT 2022-2023 (II)](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/BienRep/REP_EN_22-23-II-2.pdf)

### 2024 TAC = 40,570 t
The TAC for 2024 remained **40,570 t** because the **2023–2025 multi-year TAC** established under Rec. 22-08 was still in force. [22-08 BFT](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Recs/compendiopdf-e/2022-08-e.pdf)

The scientific basis in 2024 was therefore not a fresh TAC-setting exercise, but an **annual check under the MP/EC framework**. Rec. 23-07 requires SCRS to assess **exceptional circumstances annually** and says the Commission should keep TACs based on the MP unless such circumstances are found. [23-07 BFT](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Recs/compendiopdf-e/2023-07-e.pdf)

ICCAT’s 2024 SCRS report states that the management plan established in Rec. 22-08, based on the MP, sets a TAC of **40,570 t for 2023 to 2025**, and that according to the EC protocol in Rec. 23-07, **no EC exists that would warrant deviating from the TAC calculated under the MP for 2025**. The same report summarizes stock information as **current reported catch (2023) 39,247 t**, **Fcurrent/F0.1 = 0.81 (0.48–1.62)**, and **stock status (2020): overfishing = No**. [REPORT OF THE STANDING COMMITTEE](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Meetings/Docs/2024/Reports/2024_SCRS_ENG.pdf)

Because 2024 sat inside the fixed 2023–2025 TAC block, the scientific result relevant to that year was essentially: **the eastern stock still did not trigger exceptional-circumstances concerns significant enough to depart from the MP-derived TAC path**. [REPORT OF THE STANDING COMMITTEE](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Meetings/Docs/2024/Reports/2024_SCRS_ENG.pdf) [23-07 BFT](https://www.iccat.int/Documents/Recs/compendiopdf-e/2023-07-e.pdf)

On **what IBAMA has specifically done in the last two years** in the Cerrado, the clearest documented actions I found are:

1. **Operation Nova Fronteira** in **Matopiba** (reported **December 2, 2024**, covering actions in **October and November 2024**): IBAMA said it issued **52 violation notices**, imposed about **R$206 million in environmental fines**, and **seized 23 pieces of equipment and machinery** such as tractors and sprayers. The operation targeted **illegal deforestation** and especially **noncompliance with prior embargoes**. IBAMA said technical analysis of satellite imagery showed embargoed areas were still being economically exploited. [Desmatamento ilegal no Cerrado gera R$ 206 milhões em multas por descumprimento de embargos e impedimento da regeneração natural — Ibama](https://www.gov.br/ibama/pt-br/assuntos/noticias/2024/desmatamento-ilegal-no-cerrado-gera-r-206-milhoes-em-multas-por-descumprimento-de-embargos-e-impedimento-da-regeneracao-natural)

2. **Operation Caixa-Forte** (reported **March 19, 2025**): IBAMA launched an enforcement line focused on **banks that extended rural credit to farms embargoed for illegal deforestation in the Cerrado**, in violation of environmental rules and Brazil’s rural credit rules. IBAMA said that, at that point, it had issued **10 violation notices**, totaling **more than R$3.63 million in fines**, against **three banks** in **Maranhão, Tocantins, and Piauí**. It identified illegal financing linked to **seven rural properties** and **240 hectares of embargoed areas**. The benefited landowners were also cited for **breach of embargo** and **impeding natural regeneration**. [Ibama realiza operação para fiscalizar bancos por financiamento em áreas desmatadas ilegalmente](https://www.gov.br/ibama/pt-br/assuntos/noticias/2025/ibama-realiza-operacao-para-fiscalizar-bancos-por-financiamento-em-areas-desmatadas-ilegalmente)

3. **Broad Cerrado enforcement totals** reported by IBAMA for **August 2024–May 2025**: **R$578 million in fines**, **737 infraction notices**, **455 embargo orders**, **376 seizure terms** involving **R$54.9 million in seized goods**, **77 destruction orders**, **370 preventive fire notifications**, and **20 civil actions**. This is a biome-wide enforcement summary rather than one named operation, but it is the most concrete recent aggregate picture of IBAMA’s activity I found. [No mês de maio, Pantanal e Cerrado registram continuidade na queda no desmatamento; Amazônia é afetada por incêndios e seca histórica](https://www.gov.br/ibama/pt-br/assuntos/noticias/2025/no-mes-de-maio-pantanal-e-cerrado-registram-continuidade-na-queda-no-desmatamento-amazonia-e-afetada-por-incendios-e-seca-historica)

4. **Longer two-year enforcement baseline**: on **National Cerrado Day** in **September 2024**, IBAMA said that in **2023 and 2024** it had issued **2,768 infraction notices for illegal deforestation in the Cerrado**. The same IBAMA notice, citing PPCerrado, said **at least half of Cerrado deforestation is estimated to occur without authorization**. [No Dia Nacional do Cerrado, Ibama combate incêndios florestais e desmatamento ilegal no bioma — Ibama](https://www.gov.br/ibama/pt-br/assuntos/noticias/2024/no-dia-nacional-do-cerrado-ibama-combate-incendios-florestais-e-desmatamento-ilegal-no-bioma)
